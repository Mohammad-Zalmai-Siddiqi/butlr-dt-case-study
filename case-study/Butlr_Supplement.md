# Technical Supplement

## Industry Context
Smart buildings are becoming increasingly important as organizations focus on efficiency, cost reduction, and sustainability. Traditional systems often rely on cameras and manual tracking, which can create privacy concerns and operational inefficiencies. As a result, there is a growing demand for solutions that provide accurate insights while protecting user privacy.

## Technology Overview
Butlr uses thermal sensors, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Internet of Things (IoT) technologies to track occupancy and human movement patterns without collecting personal data. Its Heatic™ sensors detect body heat instead of capturing images, ensuring privacy while still providing accurate insights about how spaces are used. 

These sensors send data to a central system where AI and machine learning algorithms analyze it to generate actionable insights. This allows organizations to understand occupancy patterns, optimize space usage, and improve operational efficiency without collecting personally identifiable information.

## Competitive Landscape
Butlr competes with traditional smart building solutions that rely on cameras and visual tracking systems. While these competitors may offer detailed data, they often raise privacy concerns and face regulatory challenges. 

Butlr’s key competitive advantage is its privacy-first design, which allows organizations to collect useful data while maintaining user trust and regulatory compliance. This positioning makes it particularly attractive for organizations that prioritize both data insights and privacy protection.

## Key Concepts
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Used to analyze occupancy data and generate actionable insights  
- **Internet of Things (IoT):** Connects sensors, devices, and systems to enable real-time data collection  
- **Thermal Sensing:** Detects body heat instead of images, ensuring privacy while tracking presence and movement
