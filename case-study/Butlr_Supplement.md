# Technical Supplement

## Industry Context
Smart buildings are becoming more important as organizations focus on efficiency, cost reduction, and sustainability. Traditional systems often rely on cameras and manual tracking, which can create privacy concerns and inefficiencies.

## Technology Overview
Butlr uses thermal sensors, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Internet of Things (IoT) technologies to track occupancy without collecting personal data. Its Heatic™ sensors detect body heat instead of capturing images, ensuring privacy while still providing accurate insights about how spaces are used.

## Competitive Landscape
Butlr competes with traditional smart building solutions that rely on cameras and visual tracking systems. These competitors may offer detailed data but often raise privacy concerns. Butlr’s key competitive advantage is its privacy-first design, which allows organizations to collect useful data while maintaining user trust and regulatory compliance.

## Key Concepts
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Used to analyze occupancy data and generate actionable insights  
- **Internet of Things (IoT):** Connects sensors, devices, and systems to enable real-time data collection  
- **Thermal Sensing:** Detects body heat instead of images, ensuring privacy while tracking presence and movement
