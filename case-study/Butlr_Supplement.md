# Technical Supplement

## Industry Context
Smart buildings are becoming increasingly important as organizations focus on efficiency, cost reduction, and sustainability. A large portion of commercial office space—often estimated at 30–50%—remains underutilized at any given time, while building systems such as HVAC and lighting continue to operate based on static assumptions.

Traditional solutions rely heavily on cameras and manual tracking, creating privacy concerns, regulatory risks, and employee resistance. As a result, there is growing demand for technologies that can deliver accurate occupancy insights while protecting individual privacy.

## Technology Overview
Butlr uses thermal sensing, Artificial Intelligence (AI), and Internet of Things (IoT) technologies to track occupancy and movement patterns without collecting personally identifiable data. Its Heatic™ sensors detect body heat rather than capturing images, ensuring that individuals cannot be visually identified.

Sensor data is processed through a centralized platform where AI and machine learning models convert anonymized signals into actionable insights. This enables organizations to optimize space utilization, reduce energy consumption, and improve operational efficiency while maintaining a privacy-by-design approach.

## Competitive Landscape
Butlr competes with camera-based systems, badge/access data solutions, and WiFi-based tracking technologies. While these alternatives may offer higher levels of individual-level detail, they often introduce significant privacy concerns and regulatory exposure.

Butlr’s key competitive advantage lies in its privacy-first architecture, which minimizes compliance risk and increases employee acceptance. However, this positioning also creates a strategic tradeoff, as some customers may demand more granular data than Butlr’s approach is designed to provide.

## Key Concepts
- **Artificial Intelligence (AI):** Used to analyze occupancy data and generate actionable insights  
- **Internet of Things (IoT):** Connects sensors and systems for real-time data collection  
- **Thermal Sensing:** Detects body heat instead of images, enabling privacy-preserving occupancy tracking
